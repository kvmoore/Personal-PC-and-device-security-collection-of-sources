Whether we are checking email, managing bank accounts, attending school, working remotely, or simply browsing social media, our personal computers and mobile devices constantly handle sensitive information. Unfortunately, the same technology that makes life convenient also creates opportunities for cybercriminals. Many users know security matters, but they are often unsure where to begin or which advice they can truly trust.

This collection was created to bring together clear, practical, and reliable cybersecurity guidance for everyday users of laptops, desktops, smartphones, and tablets. Its purpose is to simplify personal security by pointing to resources that explain how to protect accounts, maintain device integrity, secure home networks, recognize online threats, and safeguard important data. Rather than focusing on highly technical material meant for specialists, the emphasis here is on approachable information that people can realistically apply in their daily lives.

The scope of the collection spans several key areas of personal digital safety, including identity and account protection, software and operating system security, network defense, modern online risks such as phishing and scams, and strategies for privacy and data recovery. Together, these topics reflect the most common ways individuals encounter danger in the digital world.
